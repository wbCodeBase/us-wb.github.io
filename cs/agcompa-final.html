01 Client Overview

AG-Com LLC: Quality machinery. Zero digital presence.

AG-Com LLC is a US-based ecommerce company based in Elizabethtown, Pennsylvania, specialising in agricultural and industrial machinery. With a team of 10–20 people, they sell heavy farm equipment, commercial machinery, and implements to buyers across the United States a high-intent, expert B2B audience.

When they contacted White Bunnie a digital marketing agency in India serving US, UK and global clients the problem was clear: a genuinely strong product catalogue with virtually no online visibility. Just 90 organic visitors a month, 32 ranking keywords, a Domain Rating of 3, and 4 qualified leads per month from all digital channels combined.

08 Why It Worked

Four principles that drove every decision.

What turned 90 visitors into 2,500 and 4 leads into 78 wasn't luck. These are the four strategic principles that every White Bunnie case study is built on.

01

Organic SEO eliminates paid dependency

Building genuine organic authority first meant PPC could be deployed precisely only covering the highest-value gaps. As organic rankings captured more queries, paid budgets were refined. The result: 50% lower blended cost per lead while growing lead volume simultaneously. SEO and PPC together always outperform either alone.

02

Niche B2B industries reward depth, not volume

Agricultural machinery buyers are experts they research deeply before buying. Shallow content doesn't rank for these queries. Deep, technically accurate content clusters with genuine expertise signals built the topical authority Google needed to trust AG-Com's pages over established competitors with higher budgets.

03

AI visibility is already generating real leads

20% of AG-Com's total traffic now arrives from AI search platforms a channel that generated zero traffic 18 months ago. GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) is not a future-proofing strategy. It is generating qualified, inbound leads today. The brands that build AI citations now will own the next decade of search.

04

Compound growth beats one-time campaigns

12–18% month-on-month growth compounds to extraordinary numbers over 24 months. The discipline to execute consistently content, links, AEO, GEO, technical without chasing shortcuts is what separated AG-Com's trajectory from the flat lines of competitors who ran single campaigns and stopped.

GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) is the practice of optimising website content, entity signals, E-E-A-T signals, structured data, and citation footprint so that AI search platforms including ChatGPT Search, Google AI Mode, Google Gemini, and Perplexity cite your brand as a trusted source in AI-generated answers. White Bunnie's GEO strategy earned AG-Com LLC an AI Visibility Score of 20, with 22 brand mentions and 47 cited pages across AI platforms. AI referral traffic now accounts for approximately 20% of their total monthly sessions.

AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) is the practice of structuring web content so it directly answers specific user questions at the passage level, earning featured snippets, FAQ rich results, and inclusion in AI-generated search answers. White Bunnie applied AEO to every product and informational page for AG-Com LLC structuring content to match the conversational, question-based queries used by agricultural machinery buyers in Google and AI search platforms.

For most ecommerce websites starting from low domain authority (DR 1–10), initial keyword movements appear within 6–8 weeks of technical SEO deployment. White Bunnie used PPC-organic synergy: PPC campaign data identified the highest-converting search terms, which were then made the priority for organic SEO content and link building. As organic rankings captured those high-value queries, PPC budgets were progressively refined to cover only remaining gaps. This strategic overlap running SEO and PPC as a coordinated system rather than independent channels reduced AG-Com LLC's blended cost per click by 50% while simultaneously growing total lead volume.
